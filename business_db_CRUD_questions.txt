Q11. List all companies along with how many products each one sells.
----------------------------------------------------------------
SELECT c.company_id, c.company_name, c.industry,
       COUNT(p.product_id) AS total_products
FROM company c
LEFT JOIN product p ON p.company_id = c.company_id
GROUP BY c.company_id, c.company_name, c.industry
ORDER BY total_products DESC;

Hint: LEFT JOIN includes companies with 0 products. GROUP BY aggregates per company.
----------------------------------------------------------------

Q12. Find all customers who have spent more than $10,000, sorted by highest spender first.
----------------------------------------------------------------
SELECT customer_id, first_name, last_name, email,
       country, total_spent, loyalty_points
FROM customer
WHERE total_spent > 10000
ORDER BY total_spent DESC;

Hint: Simple WHERE filter on total_spent column.

Q13. Show every product priced between $10 and $200 that is currently active.
----------------------------------------------------------------
SELECT p.product_id, p.product_name, p.category,
       p.unit_price, p.stock_quantity, c.company_name
FROM product p
JOIN company c ON c.company_id = p.company_id
WHERE p.unit_price BETWEEN 10 AND 200
  AND p.is_active = 1
ORDER BY p.unit_price ASC;

Hint: BETWEEN is inclusive on both ends. JOIN brings in company name.

Q15. Show all companies and the total loyalty points of all their customers combined.
----------------------------------------------------------------
SELECT c.company_name, c.country,
       COUNT(cu.customer_id)    AS customer_count,
       SUM(cu.loyalty_points)   AS total_loyalty_pts,
       SUM(cu.total_spent)      AS total_revenue_from_customers
FROM company c
LEFT JOIN customer cu ON cu.company_id = c.company_id
GROUP BY c.company_id, c.company_name, c.country
ORDER BY total_revenue_from_customers DESC;

Hint: SUM aggregates across all customers per company. Great for dashboards.
----------------------------------------------------------------

Q40. Completely wipe all customer records for testing (TRUNCATE — faster than DELETE).
----------------------------------------------------------------
-- WARNING: removes ALL rows instantly and resets AUTO_INCREMENT.
-- Disable FK checks first.
SET FOREIGN_KEY_CHECKS = 0;
TRUNCATE TABLE customer;
SET FOREIGN_KEY_CHECKS = 1;

Hint: TRUNCATE is faster than DELETE for a full-table wipe but cannot be rolled back in most configs.
